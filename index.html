The Seven Games of Ymir

Lore of the First Giant  ·  Hidden Across the Nine Realms
✦

Long ago, before the realms were raised from void and word, before the roots of Yggdrasil had yet found soil, there stood a giant. Ymir was his name. He stood over twenty mountains tall and bled naught but life itself. Some say he was the first to bear knowledge — that wisdom pooled in him as meltwater pools in stone, untouched and patient.

Once, in his vastness, Ymir grew bored. And in his frustration — as giants do — he did not weep nor rage nor sleep. He made. Seven games were conjured from the depths of his restlessness, each greater in difficulty than the last, numbered from one to seven, from the earnest to the nearly impossible.

It is said that Ymir imbued each game with a reward of knowledge unbefitting those who wear no crown. Any man or woman to claim all seven would rise above the gods themselves — to become king over all who dwell in the high halls of Asgard.

The Seven Hidden Games
I
Midgard
The flowers bloom in fear of the depths.
II
Alfheim
The queen lays her lineage to rest.
III
Niflheim
The peaks meet the skies and far beyond.
IV
Svartalfheim
The earth sparkles like the stars above.
V
Vanaheim
Where the fae lay their hearths unseen.
VI
Muspelheim
Where the heart rests and heats the body.
VII
Unknown
Hidden away with the most hidden of all.
✦

None have claimed all seven. None have worn the crown.
The giant waits still, patient as the void before the first fire.
